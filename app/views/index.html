Andrew Hick Curriculum Vitae

About me

I am a senior quality assurance engineer who specialises in the accessible design of high-profile government services in agile teams. With a creative mind and broad range of design, research, test and code skills, I would like to expand my career into full-time interaction design.

For over two years I have had a keen interest and direct input into the user centred design of major Defra digital services. In parallel, I have built experience and knowledge in web design and prototyping.

I am a friendly, flexible and hard-working part of any team while freely challenging any decisions which are not in the interest of a service’s users.

My web portfolio is available on andrewhick.com/web.

My skills

User-centered design

I work closely and proactively with our team’s interaction and content designers. This ensures that the proposed designs are accessible, clear and usable, helping prevent issues before they arise in development. Both designers actively seek my input and proof reading before delivering a prototype.

I am confident in using and adapting HTML, CSS and simple JavaScript to create accessible websites including my own, andrewhick.com and imageteksystems.co.uk.

I regularly participate in user research activities and have conducted lab sessions with external users. In my QA role, I am responsible for ensuring that the end product can be traced back to user needs.

Accessibility

I perform regular accessibility audits on prototypes and front-end development against GOV.UK standards, using a mixture of automated tools and manual checking. I also make recommendations on how to implement fixes for accessibility issues and have worked with, and learnt from, specialist accessibility agencies.

Agile working

I am a strong advocate for agile working, and have written a Defra Digital blog post on this subject. Outside of digital services, I have helped two traditional development teams adopt more collaborative ways of working, helping them avoid wasted effort. I am a Jira administrator to help our teams track their work.

Tools and code

Given my technical background in QA, I am skilled in automated testing, where I have applied my knowledge of Ruby and Javascript to run Selenium-based browser tests. This has given me the confidence to use code-based prototyping tools such as Nunjucks.

I have also trialled the Sketch tool for prototyping and use Browserstack and Litmus to simulate pages in different browsers and clients to meet GOV.UK standards.

I frequently contribute via GitHub to our team’s design, development and QA repositories.

GOV.UK assessments

I have assessed several GOV.UK services against the Service Standard at Alpha, Beta and Live, covering user experience, design, user research and accessibility.

Presenting and learning

I contribute to the wider Defra team by sharing my knowledge, recently running two accessibility presentations to 50+ colleagues with positive feedback. I have also maintained a team wiki and an intranet site.

I consider myself a strong visual learner, specialising in mind maps which help me to retain information and share it with others.
